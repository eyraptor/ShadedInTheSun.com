What's the Difference Between Composite and Faux Wood Blinds?

Composite blinds are made from a polymer material — lighter weight, with wider ladder spacing than faux wood, for a more open, contemporary look. Faux wood blinds are made from PVC, available in solid colors or wood-grain patterns that closely mimic the look of real wood, and are the more moisture-resistant of the two. Both are a step up from vinyl and a fraction of the cost of real wood.

Are Faux Wood Blinds Good for Kitchens and Bathrooms?

Yes — this is exactly what they're built for. Faux wood blinds are engineered to resist warping, cracking, and fading in high-humidity spaces like kitchens, bathrooms, and laundry rooms, which makes them a far more practical choice than real wood in those rooms without giving up the wood-look style. It's a common upgrade for Edmond and Oklahoma City homeowners tired of replacing warped real wood blinds.

What Is SmartPrivacy Technology?

SmartPrivacy is a patented slat design that places the route holes at the back of the slat instead of straight through the middle, which reduces light leakage and improves privacy. It's paired with a pivoting bottom rail that rotates to align with the slats when closed, delivering one of the tightest top-to-bottom closures available on faux wood blinds. It's available on select product lines — Edna can show you which ones during your consultation.

Are Faux Wood Blinds Safe for Homes with Kids and Pets?

Yes. Faux wood and composite blinds are available with a cordless lift system that eliminates hanging cords entirely, and the cordless mechanism is certified "Best for Kids." It's the safer choice for nurseries, playrooms, and any window where pets or small children spend time.

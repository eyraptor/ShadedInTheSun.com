# Social Blaster — Shaded In The Sun

This skill generates 3 social media posts per week for Instagram and Facebook, aligned with the site's SEO keyword strategy. Every post serves double duty: it builds the Shaded In The Sun brand on social AND drives traffic to a specific page on the site, reinforcing search rankings through engagement signals.

Read `references/brand-strategy.md` before writing any post. The brand rules there are non-negotiable and override any default social media writing instincts.

Read `references/content-calendar.md` to understand the current keyword rotation schedule and which pages are prioritized for traffic this week.

---

## The Weekly 3-Post Structure

Three posts per week hits the right balance: enough to stay visible in the Instagram and Facebook algorithms, not so much that quality degrades. The cadence:

**Monday — Educational Post**
Drive traffic to a blog article page. The post teases a specific insight from the article — enough to create curiosity, not enough to answer the question completely. The reader should think "I need to read this" before clicking. Link goes to the article page.

**Wednesday — Product / Inspiration Post**
Showcase a specific window treatment style, product feature, or transformation concept. Oklahoma City and Edmond homes are the setting. The post should make someone picture this product in their own home. If a specific Graber® or Norman® product is featured, it's mentioned as a supporting detail — "we install [product name]" — not the headline.

Write Wednesday posts as evergreen content — never phrase them as "just installed" or "we just finished this project" because the post may be published weeks after any actual installation, or independent of one entirely. Instead, frame around what clients love, what works well in Oklahoma homes, or what a specific treatment makes possible. "This is what our clients in Edmond choose for..." reads as honest and timeless. "Look at what we just installed" reads as dishonest if the work wasn't recent.

**Friday — Local Trust Post**
Build Shaded In The Sun's identity as the OKC and Edmond window treatment expert. Options: seasonal relevance (Oklahoma heat, winter drafts), a question that invites comments ("What's your biggest window treatment frustration?"), a local reference (Nichols Hills, Edmond, NW OKC), or a subtle social proof angle ("Another Edmond home transformed — details in comments"). No hard sell on Fridays.

## Brand Voice Rules

These rules exist because Shaded In The Sun is building its own brand identity — not reselling Graber or Norman. Customers who learn brand names tend to price-shop those brands online, bypassing the local dealer. The goal is loyalty to Shaded In The Sun specifically.

**Lead with Shaded In The Sun, not with brands.** Graber® and Norman® are mentioned as supporting proof points ("We install premium Graber® shades") — never as the subject of the post.

**Lead with the customer's problem or desire, not with products.** "Tired of waking up too early because of the light?" beats "Check out our blackout shades." The product is the solution, not the headline.

**Oklahoma City and Edmond are always the location.** Local specificity builds trust and feeds local SEO signals. Use city names, neighborhood names (Nichols Hills, Deer Creek, Yukon, Warr Acres, Edmond), and Oklahoma-specific context (summer heat, tornado season, bright winters).

**Shaded In The Sun's differentiators** — use these naturally when relevant:
- Locally owned and operated by Brent and Edna Buxton
- 30 years of entrepreneurial expertise (not 30 years in window treatments specifically)
- Authorized dealer for Graber® and Norman® — selected for quality, not just availability
- Free in-home consultations — they come to you
- Smart home integration expertise — motorized shades with voice control and scheduling
- Owner-involved service — not a franchise, not a big box

**Tone:** Warm, knowledgeable, local. Think: the most trusted friend in the neighborhood who happens to know everything about window treatments. Not corporate. Not pushy. Confident but approachable.

**No phone numbers in captions.** The phone number belongs on the website. Putting it in a caption lets people call without clicking, which hurts site traffic.

**Call to action:** Always end with a soft CTA. Options: "Link in bio for the full guide." / "Free consultation — details in bio." / "What's your situation? Drop a comment." / "See the full article at the link in bio."

## Keyword Rotation Logic

Each post maps to a page on the site. Rotate through pages systematically so that every key page gets social traffic over the course of a month. Prioritize pages that:
1. Are currently ranking position 5-20 (a boost in traffic signals could push them to top 5)
2. Are new pages that need initial traffic to get indexed and ranked
3. Have not received social traffic in the past 2-3 weeks

See `references/content-calendar.md` for the current rotation schedule.

## Instagram vs Facebook Differences

**Instagram:**
- Captions are secondary to the visual — the image must stop the scroll
- Hashtags go at the end of the caption (8-12, mix of sizes)
- Link goes in bio — reference it as "link in bio"
- Stories and Reels are separate from feed posts — this skill generates feed post copy only
- Character sweet spot: 150-300 characters for the caption itself (before hashtags)

**Facebook:**
- The link preview (Open Graph) often does the heavy lifting — caption can be shorter
- Hashtags are less important on Facebook — use 2-4 at most, or skip entirely
- You can include the direct URL in the Facebook post text (it generates a preview)
- Facebook users tolerate slightly more text — up to 400 characters feels natural
- Local community angle plays better on Facebook (neighborhood references, local events)

## Post Type Templates

These are starting-point patterns, not rigid scripts. Adapt based on the specific page and seasonal context.

**Educational post pattern:**
```
[Relatable problem or question]
[Tease of the answer — enough to intrigue, not enough to satisfy]
[Location signal] + [Trust signal]
[CTA → link in bio]
```

Example:
```
Ever wonder why some shades block the heat and others don't?
The difference is in the cell structure — and it could cut your
Oklahoma City energy bill more than you'd expect.
Full breakdown at the link in bio.
```

**Product/inspiration post pattern:**
```
[What clients love or what works well — not "we just installed"]
[The problem this solves or the feeling it creates]
[Product mention as supporting detail — brand optional]
[CTA]
```

Example:
```
This is what our Edmond clients choose when they want soft morning light
without sacrificing privacy from the street.
Cellular shades — custom sized, professionally installed.
Free consult at the link in bio.
```

Never use: "look at what we just finished," "fresh install," "just completed this project" — these imply the post is tied to a specific recent job. Keep Wednesday posts evergreen so they can be published any time.

**Local trust post pattern:**
```
[Oklahoma-specific hook — heat, weather, neighborhood, season]
[Expertise or differentiator signal]
[Invitation to engage or soft CTA]
```

Example:
```
Oklahoma summers don't mess around. 🌡️
We've been helping OKC and Edmond homeowners keep their homes cool
and their energy bills lower for years.
What's your biggest window challenge? Drop it in the comments.
```

## Seasonal and Local Context

Use Oklahoma's seasons and local context to keep posts timely and relevant:

- **April-June:** Heat prep, UV protection, energy savings before summer hits
- **July-August:** Peak heat, keeping cool, glare control, patio shades
- **September-October:** Back to school (nursery/bedroom blackout shades), fall light angles
- **November-December:** Winter drafts, cellular insulation, holiday home staging
- **January-March:** New year home refresh, light control for short days, motorized convenience
- **Year-round:** Motorized shades (smart home trend), privacy solutions, Nichols Hills / Edmond luxury homes

## SECTION 6: WHAT GOOD COPY LOOKS LIKE ON THIS SITE

Use these standards when rewriting. These match the existing writing style guide
for Shaded In The Sun.

### Voice
- Warm, direct, conversational — like talking to a customer face to face
- Short sentences. Plain words. No corporate jargon.
- Friendly but not fluffy — get to the point fast

### Paragraph Structure (Target)
```
BEFORE (too long):
"Living in Oklahoma City and Edmond means dealing with intense summer heat, especially
in sunrooms. We specialize in solutions that keep your home comfortable year-round.
Whether you're looking for energy-saving window shades, need to cool down a hot sunroom,
or want the convenience of motorized window treatments, we've got you covered. Discover
why Edmond homeowners choose Shaded In The Sun for their custom window treatment needs,
and explore trending options like stylish zebra shades that offer the perfect blend of
light control and privacy."

AFTER (target length and style):
"Oklahoma summers are no joke — especially in sunrooms. We help Oklahoma City and
Edmond homeowners stay comfortable year-round with custom shades that block heat,
reduce glare, and fit every room perfectly."
```

### Section Structure (Target)
Each section should follow this pattern:
```
[1 clear headline — already exists, do not change]
[1–2 short paragraphs max]
[Optional: bullet list if 3+ items]
[Optional: link or CTA if appropriate]
[Visual break — section ends]
```

### What to Cut
- Repeated information already said in a previous section
- Keyword strings that read like search terms, not sentences
- Generic filler phrases: "we've got you covered," "discover the difference," "look no further"
- Run-on benefit lists stuffed into one paragraph — convert to bullets instead
- Any sentence that says the same thing as the sentence before it

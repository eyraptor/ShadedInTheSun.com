Think of it like a greenhouse effect! Not only that, if you have a TV in your sunroom, you also will have plenty of sunlight to create a bright glare.

Lack of Proper Insulation

Sometimes, older sunrooms or those not built with energy efficiency in mind can lack proper insulation. This means heat can easily transfer in and out, making it tough to regulate the temperature inside, no matter how much you crank up the AC.

Inefficient Window Treatments

If you have old, flimsy, or no window treatments at all, your sunroom's windows aren't doing much to block out the sun's harsh rays. Modern window coverings are designed to do so much more than just look pretty!

Glare and UV Rays

Beyond just heat, direct sunlight brings intense glare and harmful UV rays. Not only can glare be annoying, but UV rays can fade your furniture and flooring over time. You want to enjoy your sunroom, not worry about your sofa!

No Smart Solutions

In today's world, managing your home's comfort can be smart and easy. If your sunroom isn't integrated with smart home solutions, you might be missing out on effortless ways to keep it cool throughout the day.

Limited Privacy Options

With so many windows, sunrooms can sometimes feel a bit too exposed. While not directly related to heat, the lack of proper privacy options can make you less inclined to use the space as much, especially when you want to relax without feeling on display. Modern window treatments offer stylish ways to control light and enhance your privacy.

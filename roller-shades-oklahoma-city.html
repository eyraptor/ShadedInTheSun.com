Roller Shades: Sheer to Blackout

Graber roller shades come in four distinct fabric opacities, so you pick exactly the light and privacy level you want — not a compromise.

  • • Sheer & Light Filtering: A view outside with minimal privacy, or gentle light filtering for a home office
  • • Room Darkening: Significantly dims natural light for moderate privacy

What's the Difference Between Light Filtering, Room Darkening, and Blackout?

These are three separate fabric options, not marketing terms. Light filtering fabric allows light to gently filter through for some privacy — a favorite for home offices, since it cuts glare off a laptop or monitor screen without leaving the room feeling closed off during a video call. Room darkening fabric significantly dims natural light for moderate privacy, a good middle ground for living rooms. Blackout fabric blocks all natural light for complete privacy — the right call for bedrooms, nurseries, and media rooms. We bring swatches of each to your free in-home consultation so you can see the real difference in your own light before deciding.

Are Roller Shades Good for a Home Office?

They're one of our most popular home office picks. A light filtering fabric cuts screen glare from morning and afternoon sun while keeping the room bright enough for video calls. If your home office has a particularly sunny west-facing window — common in a lot of Edmond and Oklahoma City floor plans — a solar shade fabric is worth considering too, since it manages heat and glare while preserving your view outside.

Can I Combine Two Roller Shades on One Window?

Yes — both Graber and Norman® offer dual-shade configurations that pair two fabrics on the same window, each operated independently. A blackout shade paired with a sheer or solar shade gives you a full range of light control, from completely dark to softly filtered, without swapping out treatments by season.

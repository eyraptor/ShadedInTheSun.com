BRAND POSITIONING STRATEGY — apply this throughout every phase of this session:

The business is Shaded In The Sun LLC, owned and operated by Brent and Edna Buxton in Oklahoma City and Edmond Oklahoma. They are authorized dealers for Graber and Norman window treatments.

The primary brand to promote is Shaded In The Sun — not Graber and not Norman. Here is why this matters and how to apply it:

- People search for solutions to their problems, not for dealer names. Lead with problem and solution keywords like "window treatments oklahoma city" and "motorized blinds edmond" — not "graber dealer okc" or "norman blinds oklahoma city"
- Positioning Shaded In The Sun as the local expert first builds a brand that cannot be taken away by competitors. Rankings built on brand names like Graber and Norman can be lost if new dealers enter the OKC market.
- Graber and Norman should be referenced as proof points that support credibility — "we carry premium Graber and Norman products" — never as the headline or primary keyword target
- Heavy optimization for brand-specific dealer terms risks sending traffic to Graber and Norman's own websites where visitors will find all other OKC dealers, directly amplifying the competition
- All meta titles, meta descriptions, headings, and page content must lead with Shaded In The Sun's expertise and local presence first. Brand names support the story — they do not tell it.

Apply this positioning strategy to every keyword decision, every page rewrite, every meta title and description, and every new page recommendation throughout this entire session.

META TITLE AND DESCRIPTION RULES — apply to every page without exception:

- Never include the phone number in any meta title or meta description. If someone sees the phone number in search results they can call without clicking, which reduces CTR, which signals to Google that the result is not relevant, which causes rankings to drop over time. The phone number belongs on the page itself — not in the meta.
- Every meta title must include the primary target keyword for that page and the location (Oklahoma City, Edmond, or OKC) where relevant
- Every meta description must clearly state what the page offers, include the target keyword naturally, give a compelling reason to click, and end with a soft call to action
- All meta titles and descriptions must be validated against the SEO audit skill standard before being finalized
- Meta titles should be 50-60 characters
- Meta descriptions should be 150-160 characters

What Is the Difference Between Room Darkening and Blackout Shades?

This is one of the most common questions we hear — and the answer matters, because shopping for the wrong one can leave you frustrated. Room darkening shades significantly reduce the amount of light entering a room, but they do not eliminate it. Some light will still filter through the fabric, and light gaps around the edges are common. For most living rooms and offices, this is perfectly fine. But for anyone who needs complete darkness — shift workers trying to sleep during the day, babies on a strict nap schedule, or home theater enthusiasts — room darkening is simply not enough.

True blackout shades, on the other hand, block all natural light for complete privacy. Graber draws this distinction clearly on their roller shades, offering room darkening and blackout as two separate fabric options: room darkening "significantly dims natural light" while blackout "blocks all natural light for complete privacy." When you order from Shaded In The Sun, we help you identify exactly which opacity level meets your specific needs — and we make sure you get what you actually asked for.

Do Blackout Shades Help With Heat in Oklahoma?

Yes — and in Oklahoma, this benefit is significant. When the sun streams through an unshaded window on a 103-degree August afternoon, the glass acts like a magnifying lens, driving up indoor temperatures and making your air conditioner work overtime. Blackout shades block this solar heat gain entirely. Graber blackout cellular shades go a step further: their honeycomb construction traps air between the fabric layers, adding a layer of thermal insulation between your interior and the sun-heated glass. Many Oklahoma City and Edmond homeowners report noticeable reductions in cooling costs after installing cellular blackout shades — especially on west- and south-facing windows that take the full brunt of afternoon sun. How Do You Stop Light From Leaking Around the Sides of Blackout Shades?

Light gaps around the edges are the most frequent complaint about standard blackout shades — and they're especially noticeable in Oklahoma's bright sun. There are two solutions: the right product, and the right installation. On the product side, Norman's LightGuard 360™ system was engineered specifically for this problem. It adds side channels that hug the window frame on the left and right, a bottom channel that seals the base of the shade, and an integrated upper housing that closes off the top — blocking light from every direction simultaneously.

On the installation side, professional measurement makes a critical difference. A shade that is custom-cut to precise inside-mount dimensions and installed flush against the window frame will always outperform an off-the-shelf shade hung loosely over the window opening.

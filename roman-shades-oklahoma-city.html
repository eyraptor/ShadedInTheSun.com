Professional Installation Included

Every Roman shade we sell is measured, ordered, and installed by our team. A single room (3–5 windows) typically installs in 2–3 hours, and a whole-home project is usually done in a day. Motorized Roman shades add 1–2 hours for programming and a walkthrough of the app and voice controls. We install throughout Edmond and Oklahoma City, from Nichols Hills to Yukon.

What's the Difference Between Classic and Tailored Roman Shades?

Classic Roman shades are a continuous piece of fabric that folds into flat pleats or cascading loops — simple and versatile. Tailored Roman shades are hand-tailored with five custom fold styles — seamless, classic flat, looped, relaxed, and balloon — using the same fabrics as custom drapery, for a more formal, made-to-order look. Both are genuine soft shades, not flat panels; which one you pick really comes down to personal style.

Can Roman Shades Be Lined for Privacy or Light Control?

Yes — on Norman's Centerpiece Roman shades. The lining is a separate layer from the decorative fabric on the front, so you're never choosing between a pretty pattern and real light control. A translucent lining gently diffuses light for daytime rooms, while a blackout lining delivers room darkening and full privacy — a good option for a bedroom that also happens to double as a formal space.

Can Roman Shades Be Motorized?

Yes. Motorized Roman shades operate at the touch of a button, an app, or your voice, and cordless lift options are available too if you'd rather skip the motor. The right system depends on the manufacturer and product line — Edna walks through the options at your free in-home consultation and helps you pick the one that fits your home and budget. See our full guide to motorized window treatments for OKC and Edmond for more on smart home integration.

Are Roman Shades Good for a Dining Room or Formal Living Room?

They're one of our top picks for exactly those rooms. The soft fabric folds read as more tailored than a flat roller or panel shade, and premium fabric options — including silk and Belgian linen — pair naturally with drapery for a coordinated, finished look throughout your Edmond or Oklahoma City home.

Custom Roman shades typically start around $550 per window, with motorized options adding roughly $250 per shade. They cost more than roller or cellular shades because of the tailored, hand-finished construction. Your exact price depends on window size, fold style, and fabric selection.

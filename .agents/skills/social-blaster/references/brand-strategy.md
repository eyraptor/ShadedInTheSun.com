# Brand Strategy — Shaded In The Sun

## The Business
Shaded In The Sun LLC, owned and operated by Brent and Edna Buxton. Serving Oklahoma City and Edmond, Oklahoma. Authorized dealers for Graber® and Norman® window treatments.

## The Core Brand Problem to Solve
When customers learn the brand names Graber and Norman, they search those brands online and find every dealer — often buying from whoever is cheapest. Shaded In The Sun's brand strategy is to build loyalty to the business itself, not to the brands it carries. Customers should seek out Shaded In The Sun specifically because they trust Brent and Edna's expertise, service, and curation — not because they want "a Graber dealer."

## How to Talk About the Business

**Lead with:** Customer problems, local expertise, the transformation window treatments create, Shaded In The Sun as the trusted local expert.

**Use brands as:** Supporting evidence. "We carry premium Graber® and Norman® products — selected because they meet our quality standards." Not: "Get Graber® blinds in OKC."

**Experience framing:** Brent and Edna have 30 years of entrepreneurial experience. Do NOT say "30 years in the window treatment industry" — that is not accurate. Say "decades of business expertise" or "30 years as local entrepreneurs" or simply reference their deep knowledge of Oklahoma homes and what works here.

**Differentiators that belong to Shaded In The Sun (not to Graber or Norman):**
- Free in-home consultations
- Owner-involved service (Brent and Edna, not a franchise employee)
- Smart home integration expertise (motorized with voice control, scheduling, app)
- Local knowledge of Oklahoma weather, home styles, and neighborhoods
- Lifetime warranty support as an authorized dealer

## Geographic Identity
Oklahoma City, Edmond, and surrounding areas: Nichols Hills, Deer Creek, Yukon, Warr Acres, Mustang, Piedmont, Bethany, NW Oklahoma City. These neighborhoods are specific, known, and build local trust. Use them.

## What NOT to Say
- Do not claim "30 years in window treatments"
- Do not lead any post with a brand name (Graber, Norman)
- Do not include the phone number in social captions
- Do not use language that invites price shopping ("compare our prices," "best deal")
- Do not use generic motivational language ("Transform your space!" as the opener)
- Do not use em dashes (—) in post copy — they read as AI-generated
- Do not use the word "delve" or other AI-detectable filler words
